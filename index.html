Sustainable Development of Quality Education

Analyzing the underlying factors that influence the performance of primary education in Indonesia.

Overview

This project focuses on analyzing the quality of primary education in Indonesia by utilizing data mining and data visualization techniques to identify factors affecting the academic performance of elementary school students. The analysis was conducted using Indonesian public education data with the aim of understanding educational disparities, evaluating factors contributing to student learning outcomes, and supporting the achievement of Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 4: Quality Education.

My Role

In this project, I was involved in data processing, dataset exploration, visualization creation, interpretation of analysis results, and report writing to identify factors influencing primary education performance in Indonesia. This project required analytical skills, data driven thinking, and the ability to transform complex data into easily understandable insights.

Key Factors Analyzed

Factor 1: Curriculum & Performance

The analysis indicates that the type of curriculum is related to students' academic performance. Visualizations show that schools implementing the Independent Curriculum (Kurikulum Merdeka) tend to have higher median scores compared to the 2013 Curriculum, although the variation in student performance is wider.

Factor 2: Teacher Availability

The analysis reveals an upward trend in student performance as the number of teachers in a school increases. Schools with a larger teaching staff tend to produce higher average student scores. This finding highlights the importance of adequate teacher distribution.

Factor 3: Regional Education Gap

Geographical visualization illustrates differences in educational performance across regions in Indonesia. The Western Indonesia region has a higher average score compared to the Central and Eastern regions. This demonstrates that geographical factors remain significant challenges.

Factor 4: Internet Access

The internet access analysis shows that schools with internet availability tend to have greater variation in student performance. Internet access provides broader learning opportunities but also requires proper guidance and digital literacy to ensure technology is utilized optimally.

Final Poster

Project Summary

As the final outcome of the project, the team designed a visual poster summarizing all the main findings regarding the factors affecting the quality of primary education in Indonesia. This poster is tailored to convey the analysis results in a concise, informative, and easily digestible format for a general audience.
